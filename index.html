About Me

I am Qiong Li (李琼), a postdoctoral researcher in observational extragalactic astronomy at the University of Manchester. My Ph.D. was obtained in 2020 at Peking University (KIAA). My research focuses on galaxy protoclusters, quasars, and large-scale structures in the early Universe, using optical, IR, sub-mm, and radio observations with facilities such as JWST, ALMA, VLA, JCMT, and Keck.

Key research interests include:

  • Formation and evolution of protoclusters at high redshift
  • Galaxy mergers and AGN activity during reionization
  • Multi-phase circumgalactic medium of Lyα nebulae in overdense fields

Protoclusters

Discovering and characterizing galaxy protoclusters at z = 5–10 using JWST, JCMT, and ALMA.

Quasars

Studying quasar activity, feedback, and their environments in the Epoch of Reionization.

Lyα Nebulae

Exploring multi-phase circumgalactic medium and enormous Lyα nebulae with IFU observations.
