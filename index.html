About Me

I am Qiong Li (李琼), a postdoctoral researcher in observational extragalactic astronomy at the University of Manchester. My research focuses on galaxy protoclusters, quasars, and large-scale structures in the early Universe using multi-wavelength facilities such as JWST, ALMA, VLA, JCMT, and Keck. I am broadly interested in the processes driving galaxy formation and evolution in the first billion years.

Protoclusters

Discovering and characterizing galaxy protoclusters at z = 5–10 with JWST, JCMT, and ALMA.

Quasars

Studying quasar activity, mergers, and feedback during the Epoch of Reionization.

Lyα Nebulae

Exploring the circumgalactic medium and enormous Lyα nebulae with IFU observations.
